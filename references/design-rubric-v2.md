# Fable Design Rubric v2

Score Fable UI outputs on 10 dimensions, 0-5 each. Total 50.

| Dimension | 0 | 3 | 5 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Screen job clarity | no clear job | partially clear | one sharp job drives all decisions |
| Product specificity | generic product words | some specific data/copy | believable product moments and domain detail |
| Hierarchy | everything competes | primary area visible | one loudest element, clear supporting structure |
| Layout/responsiveness | breaks or generic | works but basic | mobile-first and desktop-composed |
| Design system discipline | random styles | mostly consistent | tokens/components/palette/radius/type are coherent |
| State coverage | success-only | some states | empty/loading/error/success/disabled/focus handled where relevant |
| Interaction/motion | absent or decorative | acceptable | purposeful, fast, accessible, tactile |
| Accessibility/readability | low contrast/overflow | mostly readable | contrast, focus, line-length, touch targets respected |
| Anti-slop avoidance | obvious AI defaults | minor tells | no generic AI tells; distinctive but appropriate |
| Verification honesty | claims without evidence | partial evidence | rendered/inspected and evidence linked |

## Winner rule

A Fable output is meaningfully better than baseline only if:

- total score improves by at least 5/50, or
- it fixes a critical product/design failure, or
- it produces stronger verified implementation while maintaining similar score.

If the score is close, say so. Do not overclaim Fable improvement.
